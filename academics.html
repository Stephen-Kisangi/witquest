Our Academic Approach

At Witquest Castle School, we blend Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum with international teaching methodologies to create well-rounded, globally competitive learners.

Competency-Based

Our CBC implementation focuses on developing essential skills rather than rote memorization, preparing students for real-world challenges.

International Perspective

We incorporate global learning approaches while maintaining strong connections to Kenyan culture and values.

Personalized Learning

Our small class sizes enable teachers to tailor instruction to each student's unique learning style and pace.

Our Curriculum

Discover our comprehensive CBC-aligned educational programs for different age groups.

Early Childhood Development

Our playgroup program focuses on foundational development through play-based learning, laying the groundwork for future academic success while nurturing curiosity and social skills.

  • Development of communication and language skills
  • Motor skills development through guided play
  • Introduction to numbers and letters
  • Social and emotional development

Our playgroup day is structured around developmentally appropriate activities:

  • Morning circle time
  • Guided play sessions
  • Outdoor exploration
  • Arts and crafts
  • Music and movement
  • Story time

By the end of our playgroup program, children will:

  • Demonstrate improved communication skills
  • Show confidence in social interactions
  • Exhibit curiosity and eagerness to learn
  • Recognize basic letters, numbers and shapes
  • Display age-appropriate motor skills

Primary Education

Our primary school program fully implements Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum, focusing on developing core competencies while ensuring academic excellence in a supportive environment.

  • Literacy and numeracy mastery
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Digital literacy and technology skills
  • Creative arts and self-expression

Lower Primary (Grades 1-3)
  • Literacy Activities
  • Kiswahili Language Activities
  • Mathematical Activities
  • Environmental Activities
  • Hygiene and Nutrition Activities
  • Religious Education
Upper Primary (Grades 4-6)
  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Mathematics
  • Science and Technology
  • Social Studies
  • Creative Arts
  • Agriculture
  • Religious Education

Our primary program develops these key competencies as mandated by the CBC:

  • Communication and collaboration
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Imagination and creativity
  • Citizenship
  • Digital literacy
  • Learning to learn
  • Self-efficacy

Our assessment aligns with CBC principles, focusing on:

  • Continuous formative assessment
  • Project-based evaluation
  • Portfolio development
  • Skills demonstration
  • Classroom-based assessments (CBA)

Junior Secondary Education

Our junior secondary program builds on the CBC foundation established in primary school, focusing on deeper subject mastery and practical application of knowledge while preparing students for senior secondary education.

  • Advanced subject-specific knowledge
  • Career pathways exploration
  • Leadership and community service
  • Intermediate research skills

Our junior secondary curriculum includes these mandatory subjects:

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Mathematics
  • Integrated Science
  • Health Education
  • Pre-Technical and Pre-Career Education
  • Social Studies
  • Religious Education
  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • Life Skills Education
  • Sports and Physical Education

Students select at least one subject from each category:

Arts and Sports Science
  • Visual Arts
  • Performing Arts
  • Sports and Physical Education
Technical Studies
  • Computer Science
  • Home Science
  • Design and Technology
  • Agriculture
Foreign Languages
  • French
  • German
  • Mandarin

In line with CBC requirements, our Junior Secondary students engage in:

  • Identifying community needs and challenges
  • Designing intervention projects
  • Implementing community service initiatives
  • Reflecting on their learning and impact

Each student completes at least 45 hours of community service learning per academic year.

Our Teaching Approach

We employ innovative teaching methodologies that align with the CBC framework while creating an engaging learning environment.

Collaborative Learning

Our students work in diverse teams to solve problems, complete projects, and develop critical social skills needed for success in the 21st century.

Project-Based Learning

Students engage in hands-on projects that integrate multiple subjects, making learning relevant and applicable to real-world situations.

Inquiry-Based Learning

We encourage curiosity by guiding students to ask questions, investigate topics, and discover answers through structured inquiry processes.

Technology Integration

Digital literacy is embedded across our curriculum, with age-appropriate technology tools enhancing learning experiences at every level.
